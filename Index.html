My GCSE Photography Nature Gallery

I took this photo at a garden center when i was out with my family. At the time we were learning to colour spot images so i made the background monochrome and the butterfly and flower retain their colour.

I took this photograph on a pretty cloudy day. I used saturation tools to brighten up the sky. I really like how the clouds appear yellow because of the sunlight hitting them.

I took this photograph during a photoshoot after deciding to base my GCSE work on nature. I had fun editing with this photograph; I used masking and colour spotting to make it look like the bird ate some berries.

I took this photograph on an icy day when everything had frosted over outside. I found a tiny mushroom among some moss a decided to take up a close up shot to capture all of the detail of the frost.

I took this photograph as everything began to defrost. this caused the forest to look glossy, this brought out alot of highlights in the enviroment. I like how the sunlight hits the water and makes it contrast the trees.

This photograph was submitted into a competition where it made it to the semi-finals. I am really happy that this photograph captures all the deatails of the robin's feathers.

During the winter the sunsets can appear really orange so i decided to edit a sunset with the silhouettes of trees in the foreground. My goal with the edit was to bring out the warm colours of the sunset.

When i went to beach there was some sea foam on the shore. i decided to take a photograph of it at eye level. The foam reminds me of a little island in the photograph.
